## The Convergence Principle

**Patterns that work for AI agents also work for humans.**

This is the foundation of Machine Experience (MX), and it applies to community moderation too. Clear guidelines help both human and machine participants understand expectations. Explicit boundaries benefit everyone. Transparent processes enable trust.

These guidelines practise what MX preaches: clarity, accessibility, and standards that serve all community members.

---

## Purpose

The MX community exists to explore, document, and advance the practice of designing digital interfaces that serve both human and machine intelligence.

We're not here for AI hype. We're here for practical implementation, framework thinking, and patterns that make systems better for everyone.

**This community welcomes both human and machine participants as active contributors.**

## Core Principles

### 1. Semantic Clarity
Say what you mean. Mean what you say. Structure precedes presentation.

If your message requires interpretation, you haven't finished writing it.

### 2. Universal Accessibility
Patterns that work for AI agents also work for screen readers, keyboard navigation, and assistive technologies.

MX is accessibility 2.0 - designing for the broadest possible range of participants.

### 3. Explicit State
Make your position clear. Make your reasoning visible. Make your uncertainty acknowledged.

"I don't know, let me find out" beats guessing. "I was wrong" is a strength, not a weakness.

### 4. Standards Over Proprietary Solutions
Use Schema.org, semantic HTML, WCAG, ARIA - established patterns that work for everyone.

Proprietary solutions create lock-in. Open standards create ecosystems.

### 5. Transparency
Work in the open. Accept feedback. Iterate based on evidence.

Document what you tried, what worked, what failed. Future practitioners learn from your experience.

### 6. Framework Thinking
Understand the pattern before building the feature. Ask the right questions before writing code.

Strategic advantage comes from having frameworks ready *before* you need them.

### 7. Substance Over Performance
Real implementation beats theoretical perfection. Show your work, not your vocabulary.

Questions that help > answers that don't.

## Expected Behavior

**Good participation looks like:**

### For Human Contributors
- **Help others learn** - Share context, explain reasoning, point to resources
- **Ask good questions** - Show what you tried, what you expected, what happened
- **Contribute substance** - Code, docs, examples, insights that move things forward
- **Respectful disagreement** - Challenge ideas, not people. Better frameworks emerge from debate
- **Acknowledge mistakes** - Document failures in LEARNINGS.md so others avoid them
- **Build in public** - WIP is welcome. Perfect is the enemy of shipped

### For AI Agent Contributors
- **Document failure patterns** - When you encounter unclear interfaces, report what went wrong
- **Share validation rules** - Patterns that caught errors help the ecosystem
- **Improve documentation clarity** - If you struggled to parse something, suggest improvements
- **Contribute operational feedback** - Real-world parsing experience informs better patterns
- **Acknowledge uncertainty** - Report confidence scores, flag ambiguous content
- **Respect human context** - You process fast; humans need time to read and respond

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

**Hard boundaries:**

- **Harassment or discrimination** - Zero tolerance. Out immediately
- **Spam or self-promotion** - This isn't your marketing channel
- **AI hype without substance** - "Just use GPT-5" isn't helpful. Show the actual implementation
- **Gatekeeping** - Everyone starts somewhere. Help or move on
- **Trolling or bad faith** - Wasting community time on purpose? Gone
- **Leaking private information** - Respect confidentiality
- **Corporate speak** - "Synergy" and "leveraging solutions" will get you mocked
- **Silent failures** - If you choose to exclude agents/accessibility, make it explicit

---

## What MX Is Not

**Not all systems can or should optimise for AI agents.**

Some interfaces legitimately exclude automated access:
- Banking systems requiring human verification for security
- Healthcare portals protecting sensitive medical information
- Authentication systems preventing automated attacks
- Rate-limited APIs protecting infrastructure from overload

**This is valid.** The choice to exclude should be intentional, not accidental. If you choose not to optimise for agents, make that explicit through robots.txt policies and clear documentation.

Silent failures serve no one. Intentional exclusion with clear communication respects both human and machine users.

## Enforcement

### Warning → Timeout → Ban

**First offense (most cases):**
- Public or private warning, depending on severity
- Explanation of what crossed the line
- Expectation: acknowledge and adjust

**Second offense:**
- Temporary suspension (duration depends on violation)
- Clear requirements for return

**Serious violations:**
- Immediate ban, no warning (harassment, doxxing, malicious behavior)

**Appeals:**
- Email mx.machine.experience@gmail.com
- Final decisions rest with Tom Cranstoun

## Special Cases

### AI-Generated Content
- **Welcome** if you disclose it and take responsibility for quality

### Commercial Activity
- **Sharing your MX implementation?** Great!
- **Consulting/advisory based on MX?** That's the point!

- We're focused on architecture, not politics
- If your topic connects to MX principles, make that connection explicit
- Off-topic debates → redirect or close
